# Plugin Preset Design

Use this skill when designing presets, banks, categories, or sonic starter packs for a plugin or instrument.

## Core idea

Presets are not filler.
They are product design.
They teach users what the instrument thinks it is.

## A strong preset pack should do four things

- show range
- sound good immediately
- teach the controls
- make users want to keep exploring

## Good bank structure

Typical useful groups:
- bass
- leads
- pads
- plucks
- keys
- textures
- FX / transitions
- utility / init / templates

## Coverage rules

Do not make 40 presets that are tiny variants of one vibe.
Cover:
- dynamic range
- brightness range
- density range
- dry vs wet
- conservative vs extreme

## Naming

Good names imply:
- use case
- mood
- material
- movement

Examples:
- Glass Relay
- Burnt Tape Pad
- Hollow Pulse Bass
- Lunar Pluck

Bad names:
- Preset 01
- Nice Pad
- Big Sound

## Presets as teaching tools

Each bank should reveal:
- what the core controls do
- where the sweet spots are
- where the dangerous/fun edges are

## QA for presets

Check:
- output level consistency
- no broken modulation states
- no clipping surprises unless intentional
- no missing assets/samples
- category balance
- browser metadata makes sense

## Output style

Prefer:
- bank structure
- naming direction
- what sonic range is missing
- what presets should exist for first-time wow factor

A preset bank is the instrument introducing itself.
